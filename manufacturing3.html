Assembly and Distribution:

As you can see, many many chips can fit on one 300mm wafer.

Once the wafer full of chips is made, each chip is tested while stll on the wafer. If a bad one is found, it is marked out so that it won't be used. Most bad chips tend to be around the edge of the wafer, and the best chips are in the center; sometimes even selected to undergo extended temperature testing for military or industrial use.

↑ The wafer is then cut up into its individual chips. These chips are what get 'bonded' to the chipsets in your computer. Typically, the bonding is done in countries where ↑ labour costs are low. The facilities to do so can be quite large.

The final product may take up to 6-8 weeks to complete.
In a single Sky, Kaby, or Coffee Lake (common modern CPU microarchitectures) CPU core, caches occupy around 12.67 mm² which is approximately 217 million transistors. Impressive to say the least, that's for sure.
